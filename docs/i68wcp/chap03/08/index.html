全体像The Big Picture

What-why-howの枠組みについて最初に提案した論文(Brehmer and Munzner 2013)は、枠組みにもとづいて広範な既存研究についてのタスクと対話イディオムの分類に関連して議論しています。

An earlier version of the what-why-how framework was first presented as a paper (Brehmer and Munzner 2013), which includes a very detailed discussion of its relationship to the extensive previous work in classifications of tasks and interaction idioms.

この議論のなかで既存の30の分類と20の関連研究を。。し、科学的データ解析プロセス(Springmeyer, Blattner, and Max 1992)の特徴から(Springmeyer, Blattner, and Max 1992)影響力のある低レベルのタスクの分類 [Amar et al. 05] やネットワーク・データセットの分類 [Lee et al. 06]、操作性の力学 (Heer and Shneiderman 2012) に関する分類を扱っています。

That discussion covers 30 previous classifications and 20 relevant references, ranging from a characterization of the scientific data analysis process (Springmeyer, Blattner, and Max 1992), to an influential low-level task classification [Amar et al. 05], to a taxonomy of tasks for network datasets [Lee et al. 06], to a recent taxonomy of interaction dynamics (Heer and Shneiderman 2012).

Who: Designers versus Users

Some of the challenges inherent in bridging the gaps between vis designers and users are discussed in an influential paper (van Wijk 2006).

Derive

Many vis pipeline models discuss the idea of data transformation as a critical early step (Card, Mackinlay, and Shneiderman 1999, Chi and Riedl 98), and others also point out the need to transform between different attribute types (Velleman and Wilkinson 1993). A later taxonomy of vis explicitly discusses the idea that data types can change as the result of the transformation [Tory and Mo ̈ller 04b].

Examples

The analysis examples are SpaceTree (Plaisant, Grosjean, and Bederson 2002), TreeJuxtaposer (Munzner et al. 2003), Strahler numbers for tree simplification (Auber 2002), and linked derived spaces for feature detection [henze-1998-feature-detection-in-linked-derived-spaces].
